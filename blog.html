Fixing My Swimming!

Here is a video of me during practice swimming various events I have competed in in the past. It includes 50 fly, 50 back, 50 breaststroke, 50 free, 100 IM, 200 IM, 200 Free, and 100 Free. Some of which I repeated. All of these clips were taken over 3 days (The video is a little long, feel free to skim through it, especially the 200 frees!)

How I Challenged Myself!
I'm currently on two competitive swim teams where my coaches push me to my limits, often involving challenging sprints. I decided to integrate high-speed sprints into my practices to improve my times. Over three days, I aimed to either match or beat my previous best times, although I couldn't break any records. While reviewing the footage, I discovered various technique issues in my strokes, some of which were more serious than I realized. To make it even more challenging, I started working on correcting these issues with the guidance of my coaches. Though it's a work in progress, I made some improvements. So, my challenge primarily focused on improving my swim times, and while I didn't break all my records, I made strides in addressing technique issues.

Takeaways
While I have been on Lifetime's swim team for a long time, seeing myself swim gave me a new perspective, especially with my technique. While I always knew my technique wasn't perfect, actually seeing it allows me to really focus in on correcting it. Now that I can actually see the technique issues, I have a better idea of what NOT to do, so this challenge will be beneficial in the long run. Also, another takeaway I have are the speeds of my 200s. To be blunt, not only from the footage, but from my times, the fact is simple: my 200s are slow. I know I am capable of going significantly faster. I do not think it is a stamina or endurance issue that is the cause of my slow pace, for, to me, 200 is not a great distance. Of course it's more difficult when you're racing it, but nonetheless, I can sustain it with relative ease. My issue is a pacing issue that needs correction.
